Read Full About Us

Union Community Network (UCN) is committed to empowering youth from both host and refugee communities by nurturing their talents through the arts. We also support women by providing training on gender-based violence prevention, awareness, and counseling services.

The organization works across host and refugee communities, using training sessions, peer connection, arts, and community dialogue to help people build confidence, safety, and opportunity.

Our Focus

  • Youth talent development through the arts
  • GBV prevention and awareness training
  • Counseling support and community care
  • Partnerships rooted in Kakuma

Our Programs

Practical support where community life happens.

01

Arts and Talent Development

UCN helps young people identify, practice, and showcase their talents, creating positive pathways for expression, confidence, and leadership. Through regular workshops, performances, and mentorship, youth discover their creative potential and build the self-belief needed to pursue meaningful futures.

02

GBV Prevention Training

Training sessions strengthen awareness, prevention, and response knowledge so women, youth, and families can better recognize and address gender-based violence. UCN facilitates community dialogues, peer education, and referral pathways that empower survivors and promote collective accountability.

03

Counseling and Community Support

UCN provides counseling-centered support and referrals through trusted community relationships, helping people find care close to home. Our approach integrates psychosocial support, life skills coaching, and peer networks that strengthen emotional well-being and social connection.

Where We Work

Kakuma, serving host and refugee communities.

Union Community Network creates pathways for youth and women through life skills, counseling, vocational growth, digital opportunities, and community-centered partnerships. UCN delivers targeted programs that empower youth and women across Kakuma and Kalobeyei.
